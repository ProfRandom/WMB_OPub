# Monon Morphoform Mapping

# **Monons — The Continuum of Cohesive Bodies**

Monons are **discrete, self-coherent bodies** — objects that maintain a distinct identity from their surroundings. They span an enormous continuum of masses and internal structures, from **synetically bound aggregates** (synomons) to **self-gravitating worlds** (duromons), **transitional fusors** (transomons), **full fusion stars** (fusomons), and **hypergravity objects** (peromons).

**Mononics**, the study of mononic identity, organizes this continuum into **four superclasses**, each governed by distinct physical regimes:
1. **Synomons** — cohesion-dominated bodies    
2. **Duromons** — matter-regime, gravity-shaped bodies    
3. **Transomons** — transitional, hybrid bodies    
4. **Fusomons** — fusion-regime bodies    
5. **Peromons** — hypergravity bodies
    
Each superclass reflects a different mode of **how a monon holds itself together** — from physical cohesion, to matter physics, to fusion stability, to relativistic gravity.
# Synomons
### **Synomons** _(Sub-duramonic Bodies)_
**Mass:** < 0.000001 M⨁ (10⁻⁶ ⨁)

Synomons are monons whose mass is **insufficient for duramonic behavior**. They do **not** self-gravitate into coherent global shapes, do not sustain lithic/rheic/meric regimes, and do not express morphotics.

They include:
- large meteoroids    
- rubble bodies    
- boulder-scale aggregates    
- proto-planetesimal fragments    
- sub-kilometer irregular bodies

Synomons are governed by **Synomonics**, which addresses cohesion, aggregation, fragmentation, and space-weathering dynamics.

> **Synomons do _not_ participate in Morphoforms or Somatypes.**  
> They are _pre-duramon_ bodies and form the substrate from which varymons arise.

# Varymons
Bodies dominated by **matter physics** — density, composition, phase state, and internal structure shaped by **self-gravitation**. These are the smallest monons that can sustain stable bulk behavior.

Varymons give rise to **Duramonic Morphotics**:
- **Morphoforms** — Lithoform, Rheiform, Aeriform, Ulsiform    
- **Somatypes** — Petritype, Pagotype, Fluxitype, Pneumotype, Halitype, etc.    

> **Morphotics applies fully beginning at Micromons** and continues upward through Minimon → Midimon → Mesomon → Planemon → Transomon.
## Duramons — Matter-Regime Monons

Duromons are monons whose identity is governed primarily by **matter-state physics**: the interplay of solids, liquids, gases, ices, and exotic condensed phases under **self-gravitation**. These bodies are massive enough to maintain stable bulk structure, sustain differentiated interiors, and exhibit coherent morphotic behavior.

Duromons span the familiar “planetary” mass range — from barely spherical micromons to full-scale planemons — and extend all the way to the upper limit of matter-governed worlds, just before transitional physics begins in **Transomons**.

This gives rise to **Morphoforms** (Lithoform, Rheiform, Aeriform, Ulsiform) and **Somatypes** (Petritype, Pagotype, etc.).

_Morphotics (Morphoforms + Somatypes) applies fully here._

## Transomons — Transitional-Regime Monons

Bodies in which **degeneracy pressure and deuterium fusion** begin to influence identity. Internal structures partially resemble giant planets, partially resemble faint stars.

Transomons require their own transitional somatypes (Deutertype, etc.)

_They use transitional morphotics that borrow from both worlds._
### Intermon Series
> A transitional-mass object between planemons and stars.
- Mass Range:  
    - 4131.4 ⨁ < m ≤ 25,308 ⨁ (4.131 kiloterrans to 2.5308 myriaterrans)
	- 13♃ < m < 80♃ (0.076⊙)
- **Fusion Status**: Sub-stellar; may fuse deuterium
- **Examples**: Brown dwarfs, isolated non-hydrogen fusors
- **Notes**: Symbolic “liminal” zone; fusion is partial or temporary.
## Fusomons — Fusion-Regime Monons

The identity of a fusomon is driven by **core hydrogen fusion**.  
Morphotics here are inherited directly from stellar astrophysics:

- **Spectral Class** = fusomonic morphoform    
- **Spectral Subtype** = fusomonic somatype    
- **Luminosity Class** = fusomonic structural morphotype    
- **Variability Class** = fusomonic behavior-type
- **Population Group** = broad metallicity distinction
### Stellamon Series
> A self-luminous object that sustains hydrogen fusion at its core. The foundational unit of stellar systems.
- Mass Range:
	- 25,424 ⨁  < m ≤ 99.9 × 10⁶ ⨁ (2.5424 myriaterrans to 100 megaterran)
	- 80♃ < m < 314350♃
	- 0.076⊙ < m < 300⊙ 
- **Fusion Status**: Core hydrogen fusion
- **Examples**: Proxima Centauri, the Sun, Sirius A
- **Notes**: All main-sequence stars fall here; upper bound set by radiation pressure limits to approximately (M = 300⊙).
## **Peromons — Hypergravity-Regime Monons**

The identity of peromons is set by **intense gravity and relativistic structures**:
- **Spin Class**    
- **Accretion Morphology**    
- **Horizonform**    
- **Jet Architecture**

_Their morphotics involves spin, accretion, jet structure, and horizon dynamics._
### Supramon Series
> A high-mass monon object that exceeds the stellar mass range but is **not yet galactic** in scope. Includes most known neutron stars and stellar black holes.
- Mass Range:
	- 300⊙ < m ≤ 3 × 10⁶⊙ 
- **Common Forms**:
     - Black Holes that have exceeded stellamon mass but not yet achieved ultramon mass
### Ultramon Series
> A **supermassive** object — typically a black hole — existing at the centers of galaxies or as relics of early cosmic formation.
- Mass Range:  
    3 × 10⁶⊙ < m ≤ 3 × 10⁹⊙
- **Common Forms**:
    - SMBHs        
### Hypermon Series
> A speculative class of **hypermassive monons**, potentially forming during early universe conditions or beyond current observational limits.
- Mass Range:  
    3 × 10⁹⊙ < m ≤ 3 × 10¹²⊙
- **Speculative Examples**:
    - Primordial hypercollapsoids
    - Direct-collapse black holes from Population III stars
    - Core seeds of hypermassive galaxies
