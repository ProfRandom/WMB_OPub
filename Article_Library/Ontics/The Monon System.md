A monon is any discrete, self-coherent natural object whose identity arises from a dominant physical regime ‚Äî whether chemical, mechanical, gravitational, degeneracy-based, fusion-based, or relativistic.

- For synomons ‚Üí coherence is chemical / mechanical / electrostatic    
- For varymons ‚Üí coherence is transitional, mixed    
	- For duromons ‚Üí coherence is gravitational + material physics    
	- For transomons ‚Üí coherence is partially gravitational + partially thermonuclear / degeneracy    
	- For fusomons ‚Üí coherence is gravitational but _structured by fusion_    
	- For peromons ‚Üí coherence is gravitational + relativistic / degeneracy
 
This yields a fully formalized identity system organized into **four structural superclasses**, each defined by the dominant physical regime governing its internal structure and behavior:

# 1. Synomons

### _Sub-duramonic, pre-gravitational monons (< 10‚Åª‚Å∂ ‚®Å)_

Bodies that cohere via **chemical, mechanical, or electrostatic forces** rather than gravity.

These include:
- rubble    
- large meteoroids    
- proto-planetesimal fragments    
- sub-kilometer to few-hundred-km aggregates    
- erratics, unconsolidated clusters, and chemically bound bodies    

Synomons are **true monons**, but not varymons or duromons ‚Äî they occupy the _lowest physical regime_ of mononic identity.
# 2. Varymons
_All gravitationally-coherent monons_

A **varymon** is any monon whose structural cohesion is dominated by **gravitation** rather than chemical or mechanical bonding.  
Everything above the synomon regime is a varymon.

This superclass contains four major subclasses, distinguished by the dominant internal physics shaping each regime:

- **Duromons** ‚Äî classical-matter varymons  
- **Transomons** ‚Äî partially degenerate varymons  
- **Fusomons** ‚Äî fusion-regime varymons  
- **Peromons** ‚Äî relativistic/exotic varymons

## 2.4. Peromons
### _Ultra-/Supermassive monons (> 1 megaterran)_
